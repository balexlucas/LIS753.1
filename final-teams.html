2026 FIFA World Cup - What you need to know

The Teams

Below is a list of the national teams that have already successfully qualified for the 2026 FIFA World Cup, separated by federation. As of December 2025, six spots remain up for grabs, each contested by three or four teams. The teams in those playoffs are listed below.

Co-hosts: Canada, Mexico, USA

AFC: Australia, IR Iran, Japan, Jordan, Korea Republic, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Uzbekistan

CAF: Algeria, Cabo Verde, Côte d'Ivoire, Egypt, Ghana, Morocco, Senegal, South Africa, Tunisia

Concacaf: Curaçao, Haiti, Panama

CONMEBOL: Argentina, Brazil, Colombia, Ecuador, Paraguay, Uruguay

OFC: New Zealand

UEFA: Austria, Belgium, Croatia, England, France, Germany, Netherlands, Norway, Portugal, Scotland, Spain, Switzerland

European playoff pathway A: Bosnia & Herzegovina, Italy, Northern Ireland, or Wales

European playoff pathway B: Albania, Poland, Serbia, or Ukraine

European playoff pathway C: Kosovo, Romania, Slovakia, or Turkey

European playoff pathway D: Czech Republic, Denmark, Ireland, or North Macedonia

FIFA playoff pathway A: Democratic of the Congo, Jamaica, New Caledonia

FIFA playoff pathway B: Bolivia, Iraq, or Suriname
